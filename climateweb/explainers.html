The Greenhouse Effect

The greenhouse effect is a natural process that warms the Earth's surface. When the Sun’s energy reaches the Earth, some of it is reflected back to space and the rest is absorbed, warming the planet. Greenhouse gases trap heat in the atmosphere, helping to maintain the Earth's temperature. However, human activities like burning fossil fuels increase these gases, leading to global warming.

  • Main gases: Carbon dioxide (CO₂), Methane (CH₄), Nitrous oxide (N₂O), Water vapor (H₂O)
  • Enhanced greenhouse effect → global temperature rise
  • Causes changes in weather patterns and ecosystems

What is a Carbon Footprint?

A carbon footprint measures the total greenhouse gases emitted directly and indirectly by an individual, organization, event, or product. It is usually expressed in tons of CO₂ equivalent (CO₂e).

  • Comes from energy use, transportation, food, and manufacturing
  • Reducing your carbon footprint helps combat climate change
  • Simple actions like saving electricity and reducing waste make a difference

Climate vs. Weather

Although often used interchangeably, climate and weather are different. Weather refers to short-term atmospheric conditions like rain, sunshine, or temperature over days or weeks. Climate refers to long-term patterns and averages of weather over decades.

  • Weather: What’s happening today or this week
  • Climate: What’s typical for a region over many years
  • Example: One rainy day doesn’t change a dry climate

Renewable Energy Explained

Renewable energy comes from natural sources that replenish themselves, such as sunlight, wind, and water. Unlike fossil fuels, they produce little or no greenhouse gas emissions.

  • Solar energy – power from the sun
  • Wind energy – captured using turbines
  • Hydropower – from flowing water
  • Geothermal and biomass – from Earth’s heat and organic matter
