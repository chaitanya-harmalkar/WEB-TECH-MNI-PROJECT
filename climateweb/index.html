Understanding Climate Change

Climate change refers to significant changes in global temperatures and weather patterns over time. While climate change is a natural phenomenon, scientific evidence shows that human activities have accelerated the process.

Causes of Climate Change

Fossil Fuels

The burning of coal, oil, and natural gas releases greenhouse gases, trapping heat and warming the planet.

Deforestation

Trees absorb CO2. Clearing forests for land use reduces this natural carbon sink, contributing to rising emissions.

Industrial Processes

Many industrial activities produce potent greenhouse gases like methane and nitrous oxide.

Solutions to Combat Climate Change

Renewable Energy

Transitioning from fossil fuels to renewable sources like solar and wind power.

Energy Efficiency

Improving how we use energy in homes, transport, and industries.

Reforestation

Planting new trees and protecting existing forests to absorb more carbon.
